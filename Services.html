Money Management Struggles

Financial insecurity is a real struggle for many US households. In fact, the American Psychological Association (APA) found in a recent study that, “in February 2022, 65% [of people] reported that money was a significant source of stress” (APA 2022). Why are so many people anxious when talking or even thinking about their finances? In response to this question, a similar study elaborates, “Major factors contributing to high levels of financial anxiety and stress include a lack of assets and insufficient income, high debt, money management challenges and low financial literacy” (FINRA 2021). Finances are definitely an issue running rampant and are causing the majority of Americans an unnecessary amount of stress. Why is this? The problem lies in financial illiteracy.

Financial Literacy

In school, we learn about math, rhetoric, science, and health, but we fail to learn about money. As an undergraduate in Finance, I am particularly intrigued by the concept of budgeting, investing, and saving money. I grew up with parents who also loved to budget and who taught me many vital and important concepts that many seem to struggle with. When I began college, I expanded these money management skills as I took classes such as information systems, accounting, and finance. I built complex excel spreadsheets and greatly diminished my “money anxiety” during college by carefully keeping track of my expenses and income. Now, I want to help you do the same.

Build a Budget

The first steps to financial literacy are setting goals and building a budget. As a financial advisor, I will help you better organize your monthly expenses and revenues and to make focused driven and attainable goals. With these goals in mind we will begin forming your budget. I will help you best allocate your money and begin growing your savings. Keeping your money in order can definitely be a daunting task. By building a budget, you will be empowered by your money and will find peace of mind.
